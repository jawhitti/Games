# NO FATE — Rulebook

*A secret war fought across time. Your hero is here; your love is lost somewhere in the
collapsing timeline. Bring them home — before someone makes sure you never do.*

2–6 players · ~15–25 minutes · the only components are cards and a fistful of point chits.

---

## 1. Overview

The future sent everyone you love back through a failing time-portal, and they came out
**scattered through the timeline** — a face-down deck called **the Future**. Each player has a
**hero** in hand and a **sidekick** (their other half) lost somewhere in that deck.

Every turn, the next card crawls out of the portal into the **Present** and resolves. On your
turn you also **fish** the Future — cut the deck, sneak a look, and act on what you see:
plant a killing machine, slip your hero in toward your love, or sabotage someone else's
reunion.

**Get your hero home to your sidekick and you win, instantly.** But everyone can see roughly
*where* you're digging, and the timeline keeps shuddering forward. A friendly table tells a
love story. A cutthroat table tells a tragedy. Same deck — the players decide which.

---

## 2. Components

- **The Future deck** — the face-down draw stack (the portal). It contains:
  - **6 sidekicks** (one per couple), seeded into the bottom half at setup.
  - **Prizes, neutrals, one-shot & lasting upgrades, downgrades, potatoes, characters,** and
    **globals** (see the Glossary, §7).
- **Per player:** **1 hero** (matched to your sidekick) and **5 Terminators** (your reusable fleet).
- **Point chits** — to track your score and the shifting value of prizes you're holding.

There is no board. The **Present** is just a face-down row of **8 cards** on the table; the
**Future** is the deck beside it; a **discard** pile collects the dead.

---

## 3. Setup

1. **Take your couple's hero and your 5 Terminators into your hand.** (You start with 6 cards.)
   Your hero stays with you until you choose to send them in. Set your matching **sidekick**
   aside for step 3.
2. **Shuffle the rest of the deck** (everything except the heroes and sidekicks).
3. **Deal 8 cards face-down as the opening Present.** (No pair card can be in the opening 8.)
4. **Cut the remaining deck into two roughly-equal piles.** Shuffle all the **sidekicks** into
   the **bottom** pile, then set the top pile back on top. The sidekicks now live, scattered,
   in the deep half of the Future — each couple a different distance from home.
5. Pick a first player. Play passes **left**.

> *Why the deep half:* it keeps your sidekick from wandering out, alone and lost, in the
> opening moments — and it makes the hunt a real journey.

---

## 4. A turn — two steps

Two steps, in order. Then play passes left.

**Step 1 — Resolve the Present.** Flip the top card of the Present face-up and resolve it
(how each kind behaves is §5). Then refill the Present back to 8 from the top of the Future.
One card resolves per turn — the timeline always moves forward.

> *Reactive:* a player holding **The 13th Monkey** may, instead of letting a surfacing card
> resolve, fling it to the bottom of the Future (any card except a global).

**Step 2 — Fish the Future.** Cut the deck wherever you like and secretly look at a band of
cards there — your **sight** is **1 + (sidekicks already gone)** cards. Then take **one** action
on what you saw:

- **Plant a Terminator** on one of those cards.
- **Send in your hero** — plant them on one of those cards. *Sending your hero costs a tribute:
  discard one prize you're holding.*
- **Play one one-shot** on a card you saw — Sabotage, EMP, Hasta La Vista, The Claw, Temporal
  Splice, Temporal Shift, or Actually Fate.
- **Re-bury a Potato** from your hand, or **pass.**

One look, one action. Then if you're over the **7-card hand limit**, send cards to the bottom
until you're at 7.

> When a **Prize / Upgrade / Potato / etc.** is the card that *resolves* in Step 1, the active
> player simply chooses to **take it** (into hand / play area) or **send it** to the bottom.
> **Globals** fire immediately and can't be refused.

---

## 5. How the pieces behave

The game lives in how heroes, sidekicks, and hunters act *on their own* as they crawl out of
the portal. The rule is always the same: **what matters is the card directly beneath one when
it surfaces.**

### Sidekicks — they only want to get home
A sidekick never fights. **If she surfaces with no hero waiting, she walks home alone** — she
leaves the board, banks her ladder value (§6) for whoever flipped her, and **her reunion is now
dead.** She is the prize everyone is racing toward or trying to seize.

> **A sidekick emerges alone:** Marty McFly crawls out with no Doc Brown near him. He walks
> home by himself — the active player banks his value, and Doc can never reunite with him.

### Heroes — they hunt their love, and they're fragile
When a hero you planted surfaces, check the card **directly beneath** them:
- **Their matching sidekick → reunion → you win, instantly.**
- A *different* sidekick → the hero hauls her home for points (no win — wrong couple).
- **A Terminator → the hero is gunned down** and lost (the machine survives).
- Nobody / anything else → the hero finds no one and is spent.

> **A hero emerges alone:** Kyle Reese surfaces, but the card beneath him is a prize, not Sarah.
> He finds no one and is spent. A wasted run.

### Hunters (Terminators) — and which side of a hero they're on
A Terminator arms itself and **takes the card directly beneath it.** Against a hero, the *side*
decides everything:

- **Terminator in FRONT of a hero** (machine on top, hero beneath) → **the hero destroys the
  machine.** A hero standing his ground wrecks the whole arriving column.
  > ```
  > red #1 surfaces; red #2, #3, #5, green #4 buddy up (convoy of 5)
  > Kyle Reese stands his ground — the convoy of 5 is destroyed; Kyle survives.
  > ```
  > Five machines walked into Kyle and all five died.

- **Terminator BEHIND a hero** (hero on top, machine beneath) → **the machine guns the hero
  down.** This is the **star-cross** — slip a hunter under a rival's guy and he dies the moment
  he surfaces.

So a hero **shields what's behind him** and **dies to what's beneath him.** Keep machines off
your hero's back; get one under a rival's.

> **Both at once — the classic tragedy.** Picture the stack `[convoy, Kyle, terminator]`:
> first the convoy crashes down onto Kyle and he **guns the whole column down and survives**;
> but the next card up is the machine that was waiting *behind* him, and on his very next
> breath **it guns him down.** He wins the battle and loses everything — convoy dead, hero
> dead, and his sidekick still out there alone.
>
> **And the cruelest — a beat too late.** Now `[terminator, Sarah, Kyle]`: the machine surfaces
> *first*, drags **Sarah** home and away (scoring her for whoever owns the machine — likely a
> rival), and stops. Then Kyle surfaces into the empty space where she just was. **He came all
> this way and missed her by a single card** — and a rival pocketed her on the way out.

### Convoys — machines chain
When a Terminator surfaces onto another machine they **buddy into a convoy**, chaining down
until the first non-machine — the **loot**. The whole haul goes home to the owner of the
**top** machine (the first one out); every machine below it is spent.

> **A convoy hauls a sidekick home (real game):**
> ```
> Player 1 flips blue terminator #5
>   green terminator #1 buddies up (convoy of 2)
>   +2 convoy bounty to Player 2
>   the convoy drags home Rachel (Player 2)! → +8 to Player 2
>   convoy of 2 → Player 2
> ```
> Player 2's machine was on *top*, so the convoy, Rachel, and the bounty all went to **Player 2**
> — even though Player 1 flipped it. You can feed your own machine into a rival's convoy and hand
> *them* the haul, so mind what's above where you plant.

Everything resolves at the **moment of surfacing**, not when planted — the advancing timeline,
and other players, can change what a machine actually finds before it gets there.

## 6. Scoring & winning

**You win the instant your hero reunites with your sidekick.** Game over. Everything else is
about reaching that — or stopping someone who's about to.

**If no one reunites, the game ends when the last sidekick has left the board, and the most
points wins.** Points come from:

- **The clock ladder (1-2-4-8).** Each sidekick that leaves the board — walked home or captured
  — is worth **double the one before**, by order of departure: **1, 2, 4, 8…** Capturing or
  escorting her home **doubles it again**. So the **last couple to fall is worth the most** —
  in a game where nobody reunites, whoever seizes the final sidekick usually wins.
- **Prizes** you're holding — each worth its chit value (many shift with the board; see §7).
- **Convoy bounties** — **+2** for every machine you haul home in a convoy.
- **Characters** you've kept in play.
- **Potatoes** count **against** you at the end — pass them on.

A gentle table usually ends in a reunion; a cutthroat table grinds down to the ladder, where
the last, fattest couple decides it. **The table chooses the genre.**

## 7. Glossary of cards

### The couples (heroes & sidekicks)
Six pairs: **Kyle Reese + Sarah Connor · Deckard + Rachel · Doc Brown + Marty McFly · Neo +
Trinity · Han Solo + Princess Leia · Ellen Ripley + Newt.** Your **hero** starts in your hand;
your **sidekick** starts lost in the deep deck. Reunite them to win. A hero held (never sent)
is worth nothing — they exist to be *used*.

### Terminators (your fleet — 5 each)
Reusable killing machines. Plant one to capture the card below it; the winner of a convoy
**comes back to your hand** to be planted again. Beaten only by a hero standing beneath them,
by EMP / Hasta La Vista, or by being star-crossed as the under-card of a planted hero.

### Prizes (points — kept in hand, value tracked on chits)
Most prizes have a **dynamic value** that changes with the board, so you hold the card and chit
its worth. Over the cap, you may have to ditch one (and lose those points).
- **The Scrap Fields** — 1 + 1 per machine *you've* planted.
- **A Hidden Pantry** — 1 + 1 per sidekick already gone.
- **The Armory** — 1 + 1 per machine in play (anyone's).
- **Running on Fumes** — worth more the *fewer* machines you hold (1 + (5 − your machines)).
- **Fresh Recruits** — 1 + 1 per sidekick still in play.
- **Spoils of War** — 1 + 1 per *rival* machine in play.
- **A Full Pack** — 1 per card in your hand when taken.
- **The Power of Love** — 2, doubled to 4 while your sidekick is still alive.
- **A Comrade's Effects** — 2, doubled to 4 if your sidekick is already lost.
- **The Needs of the Many** — worth 5, but on take you sacrifice one card from your hand.
- **John Connor / Into the Unknown** — worth the current ladder value when taken (1/2/4/8…),
  so they get richer the longer you wait.

### One-shot upgrades (play on your Fish, act on what you peeked)
- **Sabotage** (×3) — send any one card you peeked to the bottom. *The star-cross: bury a rival
  hero and his sidekick comes home alone.*
- **Temporal Shift** (×3) — move a peeked card exactly 3 spaces up or down. Slip a hero behind
  his sidekick; edge your own pair toward home.
- **Temporal Splice** (×4) — re-order the whole band you peeked. Align your pair, or split a
  rival's couple apart.
- **Hasta La Vista, Baby** (×3) — destroy a peeked card outright (anything but a hero, sidekick,
  or the Timequake).
- **EMP Charge** (×2) — destroy a peeked Terminator outright.
- **The Claw** (×2) — yank a peeked Terminator out of the Future, back to its owner's hand.
- **Actually, Fate** (×2) — drag a peeked card *up into the Present* at a depth you choose. *The
  assassin's reach: the only way to gun down a hero who's already made it past the wire.*
- **Temporal Insertion** (×1) — view two cards at a cut, then place any hand card anywhere.
- **Field Promotion** (×2) — take an extra Fish this turn.
- **Quartermaster's Favor** (×1) — ditch a hand card to the bottom of the Future.
- **Bullet Time** (×1) — read the *entire* Present, in order. Pure foresight.
- **The 13th Monkey** (×1) — *reactive:* fling any surfacing card (except a global) to the bottom.

### Lasting upgrades (keep in front of you)
- **Sharp Eyes** (×2) — +1 to your sight on every Fish, forever.
- **Veteran Chassis** (×1) — your planted machines can't be buddied or stolen by another machine.

### Characters (auras — you pay a few points to keep them)
- **The Defector** (×2) — an extra Fish + plant every turn.
- **The Survivor** (×2) — your machines can't be buddied/taken.
- **The Fixer** (×2) — your machines skip junk (never haul a potato/downgrade; grab the next real card).

### Downgrades (they land on *you*)
- **Temporal Sickness** — skip your next two Fish actions.
- **Glitched Targeting** — your next planted machine surfaces and grabs nothing.
- **Static Cling** — you may peek but not plant for two turns.

### Potatoes (liabilities — pass them on)
- **A Potato (−9), A Time-Travel Primer (−3), Box of Spare Pinball Machine Parts (−1), A
  Confused Child (−1).** Negative points at game's end. Re-bury one from your hand to make it
  someone else's problem; shed one gladly to a Portal Collapse.

### Neutrals
- **The Stragglers / After Curfew** — worth nothing, but on take you peek +1 this turn. The
  cards that keep the portal turning.

### Globals (fire the instant they surface — the whole table feels it)
- **Timequake** — shuffle the *entire* Future. Every planted machine, sidekick, and secret
  scatters. The one card that can't be flung by the 13th Monkey.
- **Portal Collapse** — every player throws one hand card into the rift to hold it open.
  (Holding a potato? Lucky — shed it.)
- **Flare** — cut and reveal a card **face-up to the whole table;** it stays known to everyone.
  Light up the dark — and watch the table converge on it.
- **Pass the Watch** — every player lifts a card of their choice and shows it to the player on
  their left, so everyone ends up seeing **two** cards.
- **A Word in Your Ear** — the active player shows a lifted card to **one** player of their
  choice. Make a deal, plant a lie, bait a rival.

---

*The future's not set. There's no fate but what you make. Now go get them home.*
